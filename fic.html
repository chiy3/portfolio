Abstract

The Faculty Innovation Center (FIC) of UT Austin provides innovative teaching and learning strategies and tools that help faculty and students to achieve their goal successfully. However, due to the lack of a thorough plan when building the sites, it's difficult for users to navigate through the resources. In order to better understand how users interact with the website, I installed two powerful website analytics tools. One is Google Analytics focuses on quantitative data such as pageview, user, and traffic. The other one is Crazy Egg which provides qualitative data and more detailed statistics of specific pages, for example, recording, heatmap, and click rate.

Key Findings

  • ‚Ä¢ Inbalance Usage - 50% of the pageview went to the 10 most popular pages and only 25% of the pages had been visited.
  • ‚Ä¢ Low Return Rate - less than 10% of visitors would revisit the website and the number of number of sessions per user was 1.23.
  • ‚Ä¢ Navigation Issue - pages/session was only 1.64 and according to recordings, users were usually hesitant before clicking other links on the pages and the bounce rate was 75%.
  • ‚Ä¢ Messy Structure - excessive links, overlong pages and unclear relationships between pages cause users failed in finding what they want.

Proposal

After integrating data from web analytics and my research, I gave a proposal presentation to stakeholders at FIC. I divided my proposal into three stages.

  1. 1. Navigation and IA - Optimize navigation and information architecture to make pages can be found easily and usage of all pages more balance.
  2. 2. Content Evaluation and Update - Evaluate content to see if they need to be updated, deleted or combined with other pages.
  3. 3. Integrate with other Digital Presence - Integrate with other digital presence - Integrate with Canvas courses, social media, Vimeo, partner centers within UT and without UT to create a consistent and cohesive experience for users.

Evaluation

  • 1. A/B Testing - Only half of our visitors will see the updated content and then the percentage of sample size will be dynamic based on the difference of visitor's behavior.
  • 2. Pop-up Survey - This survey will include 4 questions, who are you, why you visit the FIC website, how easy/difficult for you to find information and other comments.
