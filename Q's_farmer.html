Q's Farmer App

UI/UX

Overview

Q's Farmer App is an app more than traditional digital marketplace. It provides a platform for farmers and consumers to trade and communication. Moreover, it aims for maintaining social ties between farmers and consumers in both digital and real world. The project includes complete process of ux research, solution ideation and ui design. I did the research part as a group and the design part by myself.

Process

Background

A farmers' market is a physical retail marketplace intended to sell foods directly by farmers to consumers. There are several benefits for farmers’ market. For example, by directly selling products to consumer, farmers gain more profits than selling it to wholesalers, food processors, or large grocery firms. Consumers also receive fresher and healthier foods meanwhile reduce the cost and pollution.

Farmers hold different kind of events for people to get closer to nature and deliver knowledges of our daily food to everyone. For instance, A small farm in Austin cooperates with schools to teach kids where our food comes from and teach them how to grow their own plants. In other words, farms play the role of educator and provide a space for leisure.

Challenge

For small and medium scale farmers, they have trouble in maintaining their inventory due to several reasons. They also suffer from unnecessary loss because of the characteristics of fresh vegetable and fruit. Thus, farmers seek a new way to share their risks with consumer, meet consumer’s needs and also maintain the social connection at the same time.

User Research

Contextual Inquiry & Observations

As part of field study, we mainly relied on contextual inquiry to gain insight of users. We not only conducted contextual inquiries on small/medium 5 farmers but also conducted it on 4 different types of target users. By observing users directly and recording their attitude, mood, reaction during real tasks, it brought us the information we couldn’t retrieve from interview. Through notes, photos and videos from different person and perspectives, we were able to discuss and integrate the opinions afterward and finally catch the core values of that users care.

We use affinity diagram to list our notes.

Interview

We conducted 7 semi-structured interview as well, including the manager of 1 medium farm, 2 farmers and 4 consumers. We regarded the manager of medium farm as key informant who is able to provide us the most insights about farmers. We interviewed 4 different types of consumers as well. Different from contextual inquiry, semi-structured interview provided us information within a narrow scale. With the information in a restricted scale, we spotted the common difficulties or problems that users would face during tasks.

What We Found

Traditional Task Flow for ordering products

We mapped the traditional task flow for taking orders for customers by phone.

Challenges of farmers

We identified three challenges of farmer form the interview with farm manager and farmers.

  • Unpredictable harvest
    Due to weather or other factors, harvest of the farmer is unpredictable.

  • Short shelf life
    The products are mainly fresh food which have a short shelf life.

  • Inventory update
    Inventory data needs to be update frequently because of unpredictable harvest and short shelf life.

Core values that consumer cares

Deprived from contextual inquiries and interviews, we defined three core values that consumer cares the most.

"Flexibility"

User desires flexibility about the schedule of farmers market. There are usually a fixed schedule of farmers market. For example, the farmers markets in Austin are held on Sunday 10:00 am to 2 pm at Browning Hangar Park. However, not everyone is always available at that time. Thus, user seeks for some flexibility to buy items at different time and location.

"Convenience"

User seeks convenience when it comes to order products from farmers. In traditional way, consumers can either make a phone call or go to the farm directly to order products. Nonetheless, there is only few or even only one worker in the farm during week day. At most of time, they all working in the farm instead of staying in the office and waiting for a phone call. Users usually have troubles to make an order.

"Predictability"

User cares about what kinds of product will they have in this week's market. Users look for information for them to do other decisions. Users still purchase fresh goods from other marketplace. If they can know what will farmers have in this week's farmers market earlier, they will have a clear plan of what should buy or not buy during other shopping.

Ideation

Persona

Based on the behavior patterns and core values of user found in research. We made two personas to represent our potential users.

Brainstorming

At the first of ideation, we started from discussing with the farm manager. We all agreed that mobile app is the option with the most potential. Next, we did brainstorm to come up with what functions shall we include in our app.
We regarded three core values of user and three challenges of farmer as our pillars. We came out of 50+ function first and chose those fitted the most in our user flow. Over iteration, we came out of five functions.

Five Main Functions

Buy and sell products of farm with pre-order. Pre-order not only allows the farm to share the risk with consumers in other form but also meets the core value of users

A section not only for users to reserve events, farmers to creat events but also serves as a platform to promo information between each side

A farming simulator motivates users check the app frequently. When users harvest something on the app, they can gain same product in real world from farmers. We add weather and other condition in the game to create empathy and connection to farmers

Users have to cook almost everything that buy from the farm. Therefore, recipe serves as an useful tool to attract users to come back after purchasing

User and can send message directly to farmers, vice versa. It helps both sides to communicate efficiently and easily

For users, it's a traditional shopping cart where they can temporarily keep their product and eventually check out. For farmers, they can check, accept and reject orders

Three Goals

We listed three goals as well:

• Design an intuitive and user-friendly interface so that users can efficiently place their order or reserve for events

• Provide a platform which allows farmers to update and sell their inventory easily and effectively

• Create and maintain connection between consumer and farmer

Based on our main functions and goals, we made our first wireframe.

Reflection

“Connection between people” is always the first priority in the development of this app.

One of the biggest benefits of local farmers' market is to create social ties and link people together. Farmers' markets help create distinction and uniqueness, which can increase pride and encourage residents to engage in community. However, this kind of connection may be easily destroyed by Internet. Thus, how to maintain this connection is my first priority. In future, I think I the function for consumers to choose products with different levels of ripeness will be helpful. I hope farmers' market could grow into a hub of a community where people can find healthy food and wealthy mood.
