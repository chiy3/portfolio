QJ Farm App

UI/UX

Overview

It is an app aims to provide a platform which allows consumers to pre-order/order products and reserve events of QJ farm and also allows QJ Farm to undate and send their products easily. The project includes complete process of ux research, solution ideation and ui design. We’ve conduct over 100+ hour in field research and it is one of our proposals.

Process

Background

CSA is abbreviation of “Community-Supported Agriculture”. It is an alternative socioeconomic model of agriculture and food distribution that allows the producer and consumer to share the risks of farming. When consumers sharing risks with QJ CSA farm, they can gain the organic and eco-friendly farm products as return.

QJ CSA Farm is a small/medium scale farm located at the edge of city. The farm practices in organic and eco-friendly agriculture without pesticide and chemistry involved. People can drive there from downtown within 30 minutes. There are two goals of QJ Farm. First one is to create stable income and job opportunity for urban indigenous people. Second one is to provide a place where people can get touch with nature and learn the knowledge of our daily food.

Challenge

Generally speaking, CSA farm offers consumers regular farm products on a subscription or membership basis. Consumers pay same fee and receive products without choosing what they want and stable product amount. If there is a rich harvest, consumers will receive more product than usual. On the other hand, there will be less products after natural disasters. It is the way how consumers share the risks with the farm. During the time when we did field research there, the subscription number was decreasing. It had brought the manager to consider to seek for some change.

User Research

Contextual Inquiry & Observations

As part of field study, we mainly relied on contextual inquiry to gain insight of users. We not only conducted contextual inquiries on all workers at QJ farm including the owner and the manager but also conducted it on 5 event attendants and consumers.
By observing users directly and recording their attitude, mood, reaction during real tasks, it brought us the information we couldn’t retrieve from interview. Through notes, photos and videos from different person and perspectives, we were able to discuss and integrate the opinions afterward and finally catch the core value of that users care.

Interview

We conducted 4 semi-structured interview as well, including the manager of QJ farm and three consumers. The manager had been worked at the farm 3 years and one interviewee had been consumer of the farm for over 1 year, the others two had been consumer for less 1 year. Different from contextual inquiry, semi-structured interview provided us information within a narrow scale. With the information in a restricted scale, we spotted the common difficulties or problems that users would face during tasks.

Challenges of QJ Farm

We identified three challenges of QJ Farm form the interview with farm manager.

Unpredictable harvest
Due to weather or other factor, harvest of the farm is unpredictable.

Short shelf life
The products of QJ Farm are mainly fresh food which have a short shelf life.

Inventory update
Inventory data needs to be update frequently because of unpredictable harvest and short shelf life.

Core values that user cares

Deprived from contextual inquiries and interviews, we defined three core values that user cares the most.

"Flexibility"

User desires flexibility about the regular received products from QJ Farm. Users want to choose what and how many they want to receive each time. For example, during summer or winter break, family usually go on a trip for a longer period of time than usual. They still receive the regular products even if they aren’t at home because they cannot cease the subscription for a short period of time.

"Convenience"

User seeks convenience when it comes to order food from QJ Farm. In traditional way, consumers can either make a phone call or go to the farm directly to order products. However, there is only 5 workers including manager in the farm. At most of time, they all working in the farm instead of staying in the office. Consumers usually have troubles to make an order except subscription.

"Predictability"

User cares about what will they receive recently. Although QJ Farm notifies consumers one day before their regular receiving, consumers still looking for more information for them to do other decisions. Consumers still purchase fresh goods from other marketplace. If they can know how many and what will they receive from QJ Farm earlier, they will know what should buy or not buy during other shopping.

Ideation

Persona

Based on the behavior patterns and core values of user found in research. We made two personas to represent our potential users.

Brainstorming

At the first of ideation, we started from discussing with the farm manager. We all agreed that mobile app is the option with the most potential. Next, we did brainstorming to come up with what functions shall we include in our app.
We treated three core values of user and three challenges of QJ Farm as our pillars. We came out of 50+ function first and chose those fitted the most in our user flow. Over iteration, we came out of five functions:

1. Items: Buy and sell products of farm with pre-order. Pre-order not only allows the farm to share the risk with consumers in other form but also meets the core value of users.

2. Events: Create and reserve events

3. Recipes: Users have to cook almost everything that bought from the farm so recipe is a powerful tool to attract users to come back even after purchasing.

4. Plants: A planting simulation game aims to motivate users check the app frequently. When users harvest something on the app, when can gain real stuff from the farm in next order. We add weather condition in the game in order to create empathy and connection with farmers.

5. Message: User can send message directly to QJ Farm. It helps both side to communicate efficiently and easily.

We listed three goals as well:

• Design an intuitive and user-friendly interface so that users can efficiently place their order or reserve for events

• Provide a platform which allows QJ Farm to update and sell their inventory easily and effectively

• Create connection between consumer and producer

Based on our main functions and goals, we made our first wireframe.

Wireframe and Prototype

How does it work?

According to unpredictable supplement and pre-order, we made orders start from pending status after consumers placing their orders. The farm has to confirm that all of items in the order are available and then click “confirm order” to accept the order. We also designed a message section for consumers and producer to communicate directly and efficiently.

Paper prototying

In order to discover potential breakdowns before hi-fi interface, we conducted paper prototype testing. With lo-fi wireframe, it helps users to focus on the flow of task and their needs. Here were some of what we found:

• The location of cart is different from user’s experience on goods page
• “Cart” section is confusing for the farm manipulating
• The location of cart is different from user’s experience
• Should search function be included
• Should there be filter for items and events

and son on.
After revising over and over, we made our first hi-fi prototype.
